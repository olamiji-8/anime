Introduction
My people una doh ooo! I like this cooperative wen I join. For Osaro Cooperative, we dey do plenty things – from poultry to egg selling, from farming to small business. But wetin go make all our effort sweet na when we sabi manage our money well. Dis one na wetin dem dey call financial literacy.
Iye Nosa na one of our members. She dey train fowl and sell egg for market. But every time she collect small loan, she no dey know wetin she use the money do. She go say, 'na small money now' – but wen month end, she no fit pay the small loan wey she collect.
Why this dey happen be say many of us no dey see the reason to write how much we sell and how much we dey spend. We no dey plan ahead or save for emergency. If problem come – like sick pikin or cage break – we dey run kiti kata kiti kata to find money.
Financial Literacy:
• Save Money
• Keep Records
• Plan Spending
• Track Income & Expenses
One day na, Apa Osadolor, wey sabi book, come teach us for cooperative meeting. He talk say, 'Financial literacy na when you sabi how to save, borrow well, plan your spending, and record the money wen they enter your hand and money wen dey comot from your hand.' He show us how to use small notebook and calculator.
After that day na, Iye Nosa eye con open. She con begin dey write down everything she dey sell – egg ooo, chicken ooo, and even pepper. She dey save ₦200 every week. After 3 months, she buy bigger cage and come pay pikin school fees.
As more members con begin they follow this method wey Apa Osadolor teach us, our cooperative con dey grow. We dey plan well, we dey save for group project, and we dey support each other with small loans and business sense. Even bank come dey take us serious because we get record and we dey pay back on time.
Financial literacy na key to progress – for everyone and for Osaro Cooperative as a whole. When we manage our money with sense, we go grow our farm, train our pikin, and build better future. Make we take am serious – na rain dey fill bucket no be dew ooo! Una doooo!
